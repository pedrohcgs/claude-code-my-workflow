RANK 1 — HIGHEST RELEVANCE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Claxton, K., Martin, S., Soares, M., Rice, N., Spackman, E., Hinde, S.,
  Devlin, N., Smith, P.C., and Sculpher, M. (2015).
  "Methods for the estimation of the National Institute for Health and
  Care Excellence cost-effectiveness threshold."
  Health Technology Assessment, 19(14), 1–504.
  DOI: 10.3310/hta19140
  [IN BIB: Claxton2015] [CITED]
  FILE: health technology assessment.pdf

  WHY: The foundational methodology paper. This study's entire approach
  (2SLS, disease-level elasticity, QALY conversion) follows Claxton et al.
  directly. It is the primary methodological reference for all subsequent
  country-level estimates.

RANK 2
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Martin, S., Lomas, J., Claxton, K., and Longo, F. (2021).
  "How Effective is Marginal Healthcare Expenditure? New Evidence from
  England for 2003/04 to 2012/13."
  Applied Health Economics and Health Policy, 19(6), 885–903.
  DOI: 10.1007/s40258-021-00663-3
  [IN BIB: Martin2021] [CITED]
  FILE: martin2021.pdf

  WHY: Most direct empirical predecessor. Refines Claxton 2015 using the
  funding-rule IV approach over a 10-year panel. The instrument strategy
  in the DR paper is closest to Martin et al. Headline: £5,000–£10,000
  per QALY for the English NHS.

Espinosa, O., Rodríguez-Lesmes, P., Orozco, L., Ávila, D., Enríquez, H.,
  Romano, G., and Ceballos, M. (2021).
  "Estimating cost-effectiveness thresholds under a managed healthcare
  system: experiences from Colombia."
  Health Policy and Planning, 37(3), 359–368.
  DOI: 10.1093/heapol/czab146
  [IN BIB: Espinosa2021] [NOT YET CITED IN TEXT]
  FILE: Estimating cost-effectiveness thresholds Colombia.pdf

  WHY: The closest parallel to the DR study. Same supply-side elasticity
  methodology, Latin America, middle-income country, managed health system.
  CET: US$4,488/YLL or US$5,181/QALY (~1× GDP per capita, 2019).
  Uses drug-price regulation and HBP changes as instruments — conceptually
  similar to the DR municipality contiguity approach.

RANK 5
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Woods, B., Revill, P., Sculpher, M., and Claxton, K. (2016).
  "Country-Level Cost-Effectiveness Thresholds: Initial Estimates and
  the Need for Further Research."
  Value in Health, 19(8), 929–935.
  DOI: 10.1016/j.jval.2016.02.017
  [IN BIB: Woods2016] [NOT YET CITED IN TEXT]
  FILE: Country-Level Cost-Effectiveness Thresholds- Initial Estimates
        and the Need for Further Research.pdf

  WHY: Extends the Claxton 2015 UK estimate to low- and middle-income
  countries using income elasticity of VSL. Provides indirect CET
  benchmarks for countries without own estimates, including Latin America.
  The DR estimate (26% of GDP per capita) can be directly compared
  against the Woods et al. country range.
